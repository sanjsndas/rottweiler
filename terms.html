  • Customized Training Plans: Detailed programs designed for your specific goals and timeline
  • Ongoing Support: Guidance, advice, and follow-up support throughout your training journey
  • Educational Resources: Training materials, tips, and resources for continued success
  • Emergency Consultation: Priority support for urgent behavioral concerns

What We Expect From You

Successful training requires your active participation and commitment to:

  • Honest Communication: Provide complete and accurate information about your dog's history, behavior, and health
  • Consistency: Follow training protocols between sessions and maintain established routines
  • Safety First: Ensure your dog's current vaccinations and health clearance from your veterinarian
  • Preparation: Have your dog properly exercised and ready for each training session
  • Respect: Treat our trainers, other clients, and all animals with courtesy and respect

Health & Safety Requirements

Critical Safety Notice: Training powerful breeds like Rottweilers requires strict adherence to safety protocols. Your dog's and everyone's safety depends on following these requirements without exception.

Pre-Training Requirements

  • Veterinary Clearance: Current health certificate and vaccination records required before first session
  • Behavioral Disclosure: Full disclosure of any aggressive incidents, bite history, or concerning behaviors
  • Insurance Verification: Proof of homeowner's or renter's insurance covering pet liability
  • Emergency Contacts: Veterinarian information and emergency contact details on file

During Training Sessions

  • Supervision: Owner or designated adult must be present during all training sessions
  • Equipment: Only approved training equipment will be used; we reserve the right to refuse unsafe gear
  • Environment: Training areas must be secure and free from distractions that could compromise safety
  • Immediate Disclosure: Any incidents, injuries, or behavioral changes must be reported immediately

Health Monitoring

We monitor all dogs for signs of stress, injury, or illness during training. If we observe concerning symptoms, we may:

  • Pause or end the training session immediately
  • Require veterinary evaluation before continuing training
  • Adjust training methods to accommodate health needs
  • Recommend specialist consultation if needed

Training Results & Expectations

Realistic Expectations

Effective dog training is a process that requires time, consistency, and patience. We help set realistic expectations by:

  • Individual Assessment: Every dog is unique, and training timelines vary based on age, temperament, and history
  • Goal Setting: We work with you to establish achievable, measurable goals
  • Progress Tracking: Regular assessment and adjustment of training methods based on results
  • Honest Communication: Clear feedback about progress, challenges, and realistic timelines

Success Factors

Training success depends on several factors:

  • Consistency: Following training protocols between sessions
  • Practice: Regular reinforcement of learned behaviors
  • Environment: Maintaining consistent rules and expectations at home
  • Health: Addressing any underlying health issues that may affect behavior
  • Family Participation: All family members must consistently apply training principles

Challenging Cases

Some behavioral issues may require:

  • Extended training timelines

  • Environmental management strategies
  • Referral to specialized professionals
